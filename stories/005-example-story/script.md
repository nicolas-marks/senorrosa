# Policia & Coke

## 8-panel webcomic script

### Panel 1
- Panel number: 1
- Visual description: Midday in Campeche. The Tacoma rolls through town with the windows down, warm sunlight catching the dust and the water beyond the road. Nico drives with a calm, focused expression, Gina sits in the passenger seat, and Jake fills the rear seat with his big, eager presence. A police cruiser appears ahead and signals them to pull over.
- Narration: On the way back to the United States, the trip had felt ordinary enough until a roadside stop turned the day strange.
- Dialogue: None.
- Character emotion: Nico is alert but composed; Gina is watchful; Jake is curious and slightly too present.
- Continuity notes: It is mid day, the windows are down, and the scene is in Campeche, Mexico. Jake is in the rear seat of the Tacoma.

### Panel 2
- Panel number: 2
- Visual description: The Tacoma has stopped on the roadside. Nico rolls down his window and reaches for his travel documents. The officer stands near the driver's side, looking serious but not hostile. Gina leans forward slightly, keeping her posture practical and observant.
- Narration: Nico pulled over, rolled down the window, and got his papers ready.
- Dialogue: Officer: "What are you doing down here?"
- Character emotion: Nico is trying to be helpful and composed; Gina is attentive; the officer is neutral.
- Continuity notes: Nico is still the driver, Gina remains in the passenger seat, and the stop is happening in the same roadside setting.

### Panel 3
- Panel number: 3
- Visual description: The officer asks a second question while Nico holds the documents. Gina, practical and quick to read a situation, turns toward Nico with a look that says she is trying to help. Jake, in the back seat, is visible through the rear window, his face tilted forward as if he is trying to understand the whole exchange.
- Narration: The officer kept asking questions, and Nico answered as best he could in broken Spanish.
- Dialogue: Officer: "How long are you staying?"
- Character emotion: Nico is trying hard to keep up; Gina is mildly strategic; the officer remains steady.
- Continuity notes: The interaction remains focused on the roadside stop, and Gina is still in the passenger seat.

### Panel 4
- Panel number: 4
- Visual description: Gina points toward the officer with a quick, practical gesture. Nico, misunderstanding the moment, awkwardly reaches out and slips a small stack of pesos into the officer's hand. The officer blinks in surprise. Gina's eyes widen. Jake's expression in the rear seat is pure, unhelpful interest.
- Narration: Gina suggested a small gesture to smooth things over, but Nico took it much more literally than intended.
- Dialogue: None.
- Character emotion: Nico is embarrassed before he even understands why; Gina is alarmed; the officer is bewildered.
- Continuity notes: This is the key misunderstanding, and it happens in the same moment as the officer is beginning to wave them off.

### Panel 5
- Panel number: 5
- Visual description: The awkwardness lands hard. The officer looks down at the money in his hand, then back at Nico. Gina sharply hisses Nico's name, and Nico freezes. Jake's face in the rear seat seems to say, "I do not know what is happening, but I am invested."
- Narration: The silence that followed was worse than the mistake itself.
- Dialogue: Gina: "Nico."
- Character emotion: Nico is suddenly mortified; Gina is embarrassed and exasperated; the officer is caught off guard.
- Continuity notes: The officer still has the money in hand, and the scene stays tightly focused on the stop.

### Panel 6
- Panel number: 6
- Visual description: Nico, trying to fix the situation, awkwardly adds another small stack of pesos to the officer's hand. Gina covers her face with one hand. The officer's expression remains flat and unreadable, and Jake's rear-seat presence makes the scene even funnier just by existing.
- Narration: Nico tried to recover by offering more, which only made the moment more absurd.
- Dialogue: None.
- Character emotion: Nico is flustered and desperate; Gina is deeply embarrassed; the officer is calm and professional.
- Continuity notes: The misunderstanding escalates, but the setting remains the same roadside stop.

### Panel 7
- Panel number: 7
- Visual description: The officer returns Nico's license and waves them off with a small, resigned motion. Gina lowers her hand from her face, still mortified. Nico sits frozen in the driver's seat, his shoulders slightly raised. Jake's head appears again in the rear window, as if he is watching the end of a very strange scene.
- Narration: The embarrassment had become too obvious for everyone, and the officer simply said it was time to go.
- Dialogue: Officer: "Ya vamos."
- Character emotion: Nico is humiliated; Gina is embarrassed; the officer is practical and done with the exchange.
- Continuity notes: The stop concludes, and the documents are returned.

### Panel 8
- Panel number: 8
- Visual description: The Tacoma pulls away from the roadside, the windows still down and the coastal light softening behind them. Nico drives with a sheepish expression, Gina sits with her face half-hidden, and Jake remains in the rear seat, large and loyal and completely unhelpful in the most charming way. The mood is warm, relieved, and a little ridiculous.
- Narration: They drove away with the whole thing still ringing in their heads, and the story had already become one of those funny family memories that only gets better with time.
- Dialogue: None.
- Character emotion: Nico is sheepish; Gina is relieved and embarrassed; Jake is simply Jake.
- Continuity notes: The scene moves from the stop to the road again, with the same people and the same general setting, now leaving Campeche behind.
